## The Composer

Domenico Scarlatti (1685–1757) was an Italian Baroque composer, born the same year as Bach and Handel. Though trained in the grand operatic and sacred traditions of Italy, he spent most of his adult life in Spain and Portugal as a court musician — an experience that left a deep imprint on his style. He is best known for his 555 single-movement keyboard sonatas, which are unlike anything else written in the Baroque era: witty, percussive, often playful, full of hand-crossing, repeated notes, and sudden dynamic shifts that feel almost improvisatory.

## The Piece

K1 — catalogued first in Ralph Kirkpatrick's definitive numbering — is one of Scarlatti's earliest sonatas. Written in binary form (two repeated halves), it is relatively compact and straightforward compared to his later virtuosic works, but already shows his distinctive voice: a singing melody over a simple bass, with a quiet intensity that rewards close listening.

The piece has also been arranged for violin, which brings out a different side of the melody — more singing and sustained, in contrast to the clipped articulation of the harpsichord.
